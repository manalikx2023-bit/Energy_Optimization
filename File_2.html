How the AI Model Reduces Energy Consumption
Three regression engines trained on 30,731 slab records and 4,344 hours of process data. Model SP is ALWAYS reduced below current SP. FCE is ALWAYS predicted above recipe target. Energy formula: E = (COREX/5.12) + NG.
M2 - FCE DROPOUT PREDICTION
FCE = 697.091 + Sum(fceZ_i * ZoneActual_i) - 0.336*Thickness + 0.018*Width + 0.003*EntryTemp - 0.008*Residence + 0.305*RecipeTarget Key zone FCE coefficients: Z3 = +0.069 (BEST lever - heating zone) Z7 = +0.050 (2nd best - soaking compensator) Z8 = +0.042 (final discharge zone) Z4 = -0.037 (negative - raise only for thick slabs)
R2 = 0.6088 · MAE = +/-5.23 degC · n = 30,731 slabs
ENERGY FORMULA - CORRECTED
Energy = (COREX / 5.12) + NG Where: COREX = total COREX gas (Nm3/hr) 5.12 = calorific value normalisation factor (COREX has lower energy density) NG = natural gas (Nm3/hr) Previous formula was WRONG (subtracted instead of added). This is the correct net combustion energy formula.
Verified against all 5 schedules · Factor 5.12 is fixed
M1 - FUEL MODEL + SP REDUCTION STRATEGY
ZoneFuel_i = Intercept_i + Slope_i * SP_i (all slopes positive - physics constraint) TotalFuel = Sum(ZoneFuel_i) Fuel slopes (Nm3/hr per degC SP): Z1:25.77 Z2:34.14 Z3:40.76 Z4:14.20 Z5:1.56 Z6:0.71 Z7:1.03 Z8:0.54 SP REDUCTION RULES (opt_sp always <= cur_sp): Severe overheat (mean dev >15 degC): Z1/Z2 -50/-55, Z3/Z4 -30/-25, Z5-Z8 -15 Over-fired (mean dev >8 degC): Z1/Z2 -38/-42, Z3/Z4 -22/-18, Z5-Z8 -10 Mild over-fired (mean dev >4 degC): Z1/Z2 -25/-30, Z3/Z4 -15/-12, Z5-Z8 -6
